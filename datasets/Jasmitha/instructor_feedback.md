# Instructor Feedback for Initial Dataset

Hi Jasmitha,

Your topic is interesting and I appreciate your discussion of potential biases in both newspaper reporting and LLM results.

I have a few suggestions for moving forward with this project:

1. You include links to the full page text but do not include the excerpt that is actually being studied in your dataset. It is difficult to contextualize or verify your tone and type categories when you do not include this information. Additionally, because you have not collected the text from the newspapers, you cannot perform any type of analysis on it, only on the classifications you assigned it.
2. Your Type category needs some refinement. What is the difference here between editorial and opinion? What is the 'objective' news and what isn't? (It is important to bear in mind that this was the golden age of sensationalist news reporting, so without in-depth historical context it is difficult to ascertain the trustworthiness of the reporting.)
3. Some of your newspaper pages are out of the given scope, for example you have a page from 1963 included in your dataset.
4. The background and ownership of newspapers can provide extremely important context for both pro and anti-suffrage movement reporting. This information can often (although not always) be found on Chronicling America, so it is worth looking into and possible including in your dataset/analysis.
5. It is unclear why you are using a LLM for the search process here. Chronicling America has a keyword search function. For determining keywords to search, you could use a LLM to get started but it would be more effective to read these articles and glean contemporary language used in this kind of reporting and then refine your search based on that.
6. You need to have a concrete plan for scaling this project up for the final. Fortunately, Chronicling America has an open API, so this should not be a struggle but it is something you need to plan ahead for.

If you have any questions, please reach out to me.
